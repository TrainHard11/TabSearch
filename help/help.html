Extension Help & Keymaps

This guide provides an overview of the different views and functionalities available in the extension, along with their respective keyboard shortcuts. It aims to help you navigate and manage your Chrome tabs and bookmarks more efficiently.

Quick Search View

The **Quick Search View** is your primary entry point for rapid navigation. Its purpose is to provide a single, unified search bar to quickly find and switch between any of your **open Browser tabs** across all windows or to instantly access your **bookmarks** (if enabled in settings).

What you can do:

  • Search for tabs or bookmark (if added) by title or URL.
  • Switch to the highlighted tab, or open it if it isn’t already open in case of bookmarks
  • Close individual tabs.
  • Close all filtered tabs.

Keymaps:

  • ArrowDown: Move selection down.
  • ArrowUp : Move selection up.
  • Enter:
    • Switch to the selected tab.
    • Open the selected bookmark.
    • If no results and query exists: Search Google (if enabled in settings).
  • Delete or Ctrl + D: Close the currently highlighted tab (does not apply to bookmarks).
  • Ctrl + Shift + D: Close all currently filtered tabs (does not apply to bookmarks).
  • Ctrl + 1 to Ctrl + 4: Move the highlighted tab or open the highlighted bookmark in the current window at positions 1-4 respectively.

Settings View

The **Settings View** is where you can customize the core behavior of the extension to best suit your workflow.

What you can do:

  • Enable or disable the Web Navigator feature (Google search results).
  • Control whether a Google search is performed if no results are found in the "Quick Search" View
  • Include or exclude bookmarks from the main Tab Search.
  • Choose to always show the search input in the Bookmarks view.
  • Configure whether the popup closes after moving a tab using 0-9 keys in Tab Management.
  • Set up custom "quick tabs" for direct access via global commands.

Keymaps:

  • F1: Toggle Settings View.
  • F6: Open Browser Extention keymap assignment menu.

Related Global Commands:

  • Custom_Tab_1: Opens or switches to the URL configured for "Custom Tab 1".
  • Custom_Tab_2: Opens or switches to the URL configured for "Custom Tab 2".
  • Custom_Tab_3: Opens or switches to the URL configured for "Custom Tab 3".
  • Custom_Tab_4: Opens or switches to the URL configured for "Custom Tab 4".
  • Custom_Tab_5: Opens or switches to the URL configured for "Custom Tab 5".
  • Custom_Tab_6: Opens or switches to the URL configured for "Custom Tab 6".
  • Custom_Tab_7: Opens or switches to the URL configured for "Custom Tab 7".

Bookmarks View

The **Bookmarks View** lets you easily manage and search through your custom bookmarks added to the extension. You can also sync it with your browser’s existing bookmarks for quick access and better organization.

From here, you can choose which bookmarks you want to include in the tab search — making them searchable just like your open tabs. If you open a bookmark from this view, it will either switch to the already open tab (if it’s open), or open a new one if it’s not.

What you can do:

  • Search for bookmarks by name or URL.
  • Open bookmarks in a new tab or switch to them if already open.
  • Sync with your browser’s existing bookmarks.
  • View and manage custom bookmarks added through the extension.
  • Add new bookmarks directly from the popup (if enabled in settings) / or by a global command .
  • Delete existing bookmarks.
  • Mark specific bookmarks as searchable within the Tab Search view.

Keymaps:

  • F2: Toggle the Marks (Bookmarks) View.
  • /: Focus the search bar to filter bookmarks by name or URL.
  • ArrowUp / ArrowDown, Ctrl+K / Ctrl+J, or K / J (when focused): Move selection up or down the bookmark list.
  • Shift+K / Shift+J (or K / J with Shift): Move the selected bookmark up or down in the list order.
  • Enter: Open the selected bookmark (in a new tab or switch to it if already open).
  • Delete, D (when focused), or Ctrl+D: Delete the selected bookmark.

Related Global Commands:

  • Bookmarks_open: Directly opens the Marks (Bookmarks) view when the extension popup is launched via this command.
  • Bookmark_add: Adds the currently active browser tab as a new bookmark.

Harpoon View

The **Harpoon View** is designed for fast, efficient navigation between a small set of high-priority tabs you’re actively working with during a session. In most workflows, you're often juggling 2–4 key tabs repeatedly — Harpoon makes switching between them instant and effortless.

Think of it as a **persistent, session-focused tab switcher**. You can "harpoon" (pin) up to four important links to specific numbered slots—usually accessed with shortcuts like Alt+1 through Alt+4.

Once a tab is assigned to a slot (e.g., Harpoon 1), you can always jump to it with its shortcut—no matter where the tab is in your browser. Even if you close the tab or change its content, the slot stays mapped to the assigned URL. You can update the URL or reassign slots at any time, and the shortcut will continue to point to the updated destination.

What you can do:

  • View and manage your active harpooned tabs.
  • Jump to harpooned tabs via shortcuts (Alt+1–Alt+4).
  • Add or update a slot with the current tab.
  • Reorder or remove harpooned tabs as needed.
  • If the tab is open, switch to it; if not, open it in a new tab.

Keymaps:

  • F3: Toggle Harpoon View.
  • J / K or ArrowDown / ArrowUp: Navigate the Harpoon list.
  • Shift+J / Shift+K: Move selected item down or up in the list.
  • Enter: Open the selected tab or switch to it if already open.
  • D, Ctrl+D, or Delete: Remove the selected item.
  • Ctrl+T: Open full Harpoon view (includes Work & Fun lists).
  • Ctrl+1 (in full view): Load the Work list into the main Harpoon list.
  • Ctrl+2 (in full view): Load the Fun list into the main Harpoon list.
  • Ctrl+Alt+1: Save current Harpoon list as the new Work list.
  • Ctrl+Alt+2: Save current Harpoon list as the new Fun list.

Related Global Commands:

  • Harpoon_open: Directly opens the Harpoon view when the extension popup is launched via this command.
  • Harpoon_add: Adds the currently active tab to your Harpoon list.
  • Harpoon_1: Activates (switches to) the first harpooned tab.
  • Harpoon_2: Activates (switches to) the second harpooned tab.
  • Harpoon_3: Activates (switches to) the third harpooned tab.
  • Harpoon_4: Activates (switches to) the fourth harpooned tab.

Tab Management View

The **Tab Management View** is designed for full control over your open tabs and browser windows. Its primary purpose is to help you organize your workspace by allowing you to easily **move active tabs between existing windows** or even create a **brand new window** for a tab.

What you can do:

  • View all open Chrome windows and their tabs.
  • Move the currently active tab to any other open window.
  • Move the active tab to a brand new window.
  • Close entire Browser instances
  • Reposition the active tab within its current window.

Keymaps:

  • F4: Toggle Tab Management View.
  • Enter: Move the active tab to the selected browser instance (or open a new one).
  • Ctrl+D: Close the selected instance (except "New Empty Window").
  • H / L: Move the active tab one position left or right.
  • 1 – 9: Move the tab to that specific position (1st to 9th) in its window.
  • 0: Move the tab to the last position in its window.

Related Global Commands:

  • Tab_Management_open: Directly opens the Tab Management view when the extension popup is launched via this command.
  • Move_tab_to_1st: Moves the currently active tab to the first position in its window.
  • Move_tab_to_2nd: Moves the currently active tab to the second position in its window.
  • Move_tab_to_3rd: Moves the currently active tab to the third position in its window.
  • Move_tab_to_4th: Moves the currently active tab to the fourth position in its window.

General Global Commands

These are powerful global commands provided by the extension that you can assign custom keyboard shortcuts to. Unlike view-specific keymaps, these can be triggered from anywhere in your browser.

To bind these commands:
1. Go to chrome://extensions/shortcuts for Chromium or about:addons for Mozzila.
2. Find the extension, "Fuzzy Tab & Search Navigator".
3. Click the pencil icon next to each command and enter your desired keymap.

The remaining available commands:

  • Open_Main_View: Opens the extension popup, defaulting to the Quick Search View. This is often the primary way to launch the extension.
  • New_empty_tab: Opens a new, empty tab in your current window.
  • Cycle_tabs_playing_audio: Cycles through your open tabs that are currently playing audio, helping you quickly find the source of sound.
  • Cycle_YouTube_tabs: Cycles specifically through your open tabs that have YouTube content.
  • Move_tab_left: Moves the currently active tab one position to the left within its current window.
  • Move_tab_right: Moves the currently active tab one position to the right within its current window.
  • Cycle_media_tabs: Cycles through tabs that are currently playing any type of media (audio or video), a broader option than just YouTube.
  • Toggle_last_tab: Quickly switches between your current tab and the tab you were on immediately before, making it easy to jump back and forth between two key tabs.
