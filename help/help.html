Extension Help & Keymaps

This guide provides an overview of the different views and functionalities available in the extension, along with their respective keyboard shortcuts. It aims to help you navigate and manage your Chrome tabs and bookmarks more efficiently.

Quick Search View

The **Quick Search View** is your primary entry point for rapid navigation. Its purpose is to provide a single, unified search bar to quickly find and switch between any of your **open Chrome tabs** across all windows or to instantly access your **bookmarks** (if enabled in settings). It's designed for speed and efficiency, helping you cut down on time spent manually searching for tabs or navigating to frequently visited sites.

What you can do:

  • Search for tabs or bookmark (if added) by title or URL.
  • Switch to a tab or open a bookmark.
  • Close individual tabs.
  • Close all filtered tabs.
  • Move the highlighted tab/bookmark to a specific position in the current window.

Keymaps:

  • ArrowDown or J (when Alt not pressed): Move selection down.
  • ArrowUp or K (when Alt not pressed): Move selection up.
  • Enter:
    • Switch to the selected tab.
    • Open the selected bookmark.
    • If no results and query exists: Search Google (if enabled in settings).
    • If no selection and no query: Close the popup.
  • Delete or Ctrl + D: Close the currently highlighted tab (does not apply to bookmarks).
  • Ctrl + Shift + D: Close all currently filtered tabs (does not apply to bookmarks).
  • Ctrl + 1 to Ctrl + 4: Move the highlighted tab or open the highlighted bookmark in the current window at positions 1-4 respectively.

Settings View

The **Settings View** is where you can customize the core behavior of the extension to best suit your workflow. Adjust these options to tailor your experience, from how search results behave to setting up custom quick-access tabs.

What you can do:

  • Enable or disable the Web Navigator feature (Google search results).
  • Control whether a Google search is performed if no results are found in tabs/bookmarks.
  • Include or exclude bookmarks from the main Tab Search.
  • Allow adding new bookmarks directly from the popup.
  • Choose to always show the search input in the Bookmarks view.
  • Configure whether the popup closes after moving a tab using 0-9 keys in Tab Management.
  • Set up custom "quick tabs" for direct access via global commands.

Keymaps:

  • F1: Toggle Settings View.
  • (No specific navigation keymaps within settings; use mouse/trackpad to interact with options).

Related Global Commands:

  • Custom_Tab_1: Opens or switches to the URL configured for "Custom Tab 1" in the extension's Settings view.
  • Custom_Tab_2: Opens or switches to the URL configured for "Custom Tab 2" in the extension's Settings view.
  • Custom_Tab_3: Opens or switches to the URL configured for "Custom Tab 3" in the extension's Settings view.
  • Custom_Tab_4: Opens or switches to the URL configured for "Custom Tab 4" in the extension's Settings view.
  • Custom_Tab_5: Opens or switches to the URL configured for "Custom Tab 5" in the extension's Settings view.
  • Custom_Tab_6: Opens or switches to the URL configured for "Custom Tab 6" in the extension's Settings view.
  • Custom_Tab_7: Opens or switches to the URL configured for "Custom Tab 7" in the extension's Settings view.

Harpoon View

The **Harpoon View** empowers you to create your own set of highly accessible, "persisting" quick-access tabs. The core idea is to let you **"harpoon"** your most critical tabs – often the 1-4 tabs you work with constantly – and assign them a fixed, unchanging quick-access keymap. This means you can swiftly switch between these crucial tabs without having to search for them, regardless of where they are in your browser.

A key advantage of Harpoon is its **persistence**: once a keymap is set for a harpooned position (e.g., Harpoon_1), that keymap will always point to the URL you've assigned to that position, even if you close and reopen the tab or change the tab's content. You can update the associated link for a harpooned position, and the global keymap will automatically adapt to point to the new location. If the harpooned link isn't currently open in your browser, using the keymap will open it in a new tab; if it's already open, you'll be taken directly to it.

What you can do:

  • View your harpooned tabs.
  • Open or switch to a harpooned tab.
  • Add the currently active tab to your harpoon list.
  • Remove tabs from your harpoon list.
  • Reorder harpooned tabs.

Keymaps:

  • F3: Toggle Harpoon View.
  • ArrowDown or J (when Alt not pressed): Move selection down within the Harpoon list.
  • ArrowUp or K (when Alt not pressed): Move selection up within the Harpoon list.
  • Enter: Open or switch to the selected harpooned tab.
  • D or Delete: Remove the selected tab from Harpoon.
  • A: Add the currently active browser tab to the Harpoon list.
  • Ctrl + 1 to Ctrl + 9: Quickly activate the harpooned tab at the corresponding position.

Related Global Commands:

  • Harpoon_open: Directly opens the Harpoon view when the extension popup is launched via this command.
  • Harpoon_add: Adds the currently active tab to your Harpoon list.
  • Harpoon_1: Activates (switches to) the first harpooned tab.
  • Harpoon_2: Activates (switches to) the second harpooned tab.
  • Harpoon_3: Activates (switches to) the third harpooned tab.
  • Harpoon_4: Activates (switches to) the fourth harpooned tab.

Marks (Bookmarks) View

The **Marks (Bookmarks) View** provides a dedicated, powerful interface for searching and managing your saved bookmarks. While you can search bookmarks via the Quick Search View, this dedicated view offers more granular control, especially for bookmarks you've specifically marked as searchable within your browser. Its purpose is to allow you to **sync with your existing browser bookmarks** and provide quick access and organization.

Similar to Harpoon, if a bookmark's link isn't currently open, activating it will open it in a new tab. If it's already open, you'll be instantly navigated to that tab.

What you can do:

  • Search for bookmarks by name or URL.
  • Open bookmarks.
  • Add new bookmarks from the popup (if enabled in settings).
  • Delete existing bookmarks.

Keymaps:

  • F2: Toggle Marks (Bookmarks) View.
  • ArrowDown or J (when Alt not pressed): Move selection down within the Marks list.
  • ArrowUp or K (when Alt not pressed): Move selection up within the Marks list.
  • Enter: Open the selected bookmark.
  • Delete or D: Delete the selected bookmark.
  • A: Add the currently active browser tab as a new bookmark (if enabled).

Related Global Commands:

  • Bookmarks_open: Directly opens the Marks (Bookmarks) view when the extension popup is launched via this command.
  • Bookmark_add: Adds the currently active browser tab as a new bookmark.

Tab Management View

The **Tab Management View** is designed for comprehensive control over your open tabs and browser windows. Its primary purpose is to help you organize your workspace by allowing you to easily **move active tabs between existing windows** or even create a **brand new window** for a tab. This is invaluable when you need to declutter a window, group related tabs, or initiate a new focused work session.

What you can do:

  • View all your open Chrome windows and their contained tabs.
  • Move the currently active tab to any other open window.
  • Move the currently active tab to a brand new window.
  • Close entire windows.
  • Move the current tab to a specific position (index) within its current window.

Keymaps:

  • F6: Toggle Tab Management View.
  • ArrowDown or J (when Alt not pressed): Move window selection down.
  • ArrowUp or K (when Alt not pressed): Move window selection up.
  • Enter: Move the active tab to the selected window (or create a new window if that option is selected).
  • Ctrl + D: Close the currently selected window (does not apply to "New Empty Window" option).
  • H: Move the currently active tab to the leftmost position (index 0) in its current window.
  • L: Move the currently active tab to the rightmost position (last index) in its current window.
  • 1 to 9: Move the currently active tab to the corresponding position (1st to 9th) in its current window.
  • 0: Move the currently active tab to the last position in its current window.

Related Global Commands:

  • Tab_Management_open: Directly opens the Tab Management view when the extension popup is launched via this command.
  • Move_tab_to_1st: Moves the currently active tab to the first position in its window.
  • Move_tab_to_2nd: Moves the currently active tab to the second position in its window.
  • Move_tab_to_3rd: Moves the currently active tab to the third position in its window.
  • Move_tab_to_4th: Moves the currently active tab to the fourth position in its window.

General Global Commands (Bindable in Chrome)

These are powerful global commands provided by the extension that you can assign custom keyboard shortcuts to. Unlike view-specific keymaps, these can be triggered from anywhere in Chrome, not just when the extension popup is open. They offer quick access to core browser functions or general extension actions.

**Important:** Chrome allows assigning global shortcuts to only a limited number of commands (typically 4). Choose your most critical commands wisely!

To bind these commands:
1. Go to chrome://extensions/shortcuts.
2. Find your extension, "Fuzzy Tab & Search Navigator".
3. Click the pencil icon next to each command and enter your desired shortcut.

Available Commands:

  • Open_Main_View: Opens the extension popup, defaulting to the Quick Search View. This is often the primary way to launch the extension.
  • New_empty_tab: Opens a new, empty tab in your current window.
  • Cycle_tabs_playing_audio: Cycles through your open tabs that are currently playing audio, helping you quickly find the source of sound.
  • Cycle_YouTube_tabs: Cycles specifically through your open tabs that have YouTube content, ideal for YouTube power users.
  • Move_tab_left: Moves the currently active tab one position to the left within its current window.
  • Move_tab_right: Moves the currently active tab one position to the right within its current window.
  • Cycle_media_tabs: Cycles through tabs that are currently playing any type of media (audio or video), a broader option than just YouTube.
  • Toggle_last_tab: Quickly switches between your current tab and the tab you were on immediately before, making it easy to jump back and forth between two key tabs.

Press F4 again to hide help.
